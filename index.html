Articles

Japanese Occupation

The Japanese occupied the Philippines from 1942 to 1945, imposing strict control and affecting civilians' daily lives.

Bataan Death March

Thousands of Filipino and American soldiers were forced to march under brutal conditions after the fall of Bataan, causing high casualties.

Leyte Landing

General Douglas MacArthur returned to liberate the Philippines, marking the start of Japan's defeat in the islands.

Liberation of Manila

After intense battles, Manila was liberated from Japanese forces, restoring freedom to the city.

End of WWII

Japan surrendered, officially ending World War II in the Philippines in September 1945.
